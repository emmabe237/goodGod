Professor Jane Naana Opoku-Agyemang

Professor of Literature

Professor Jane Naana Opoku-Agyemang is a distinguished Ghanaian academic, author, and politician, renowned for her trailblazing roles in education and governance. Early Life and Education: Born on November 22, 1951, in Cape Coast, Ghana, she pursued her secondary education at Wesley Girls' High School. She earned a Bachelor of Education in English and French from the University of Cape Coast in 1977. Furthering her studies, she obtained a Master's degree and a Ph.D. from York University in Toronto, Canada, in 1980 and 1986, respectively. EN.WIKIPEDIA.ORG

Academic Career and Achievements:
Professor Opoku-Agyemang's tenure at the University of Cape Coast (UCC) began in 1986, where she held several key positions: Head of the Department of English Dean of the Faculty of Arts Founding Dean of the School of Graduate Studies and Research In 2008, she made history as the first female Vice-Chancellor of UCC, a role she held until 2012. Her leadership was marked by significant advancements in academic programs and institutional development. EN.WIKIPEDIA.ORG Contributions as Minister of Education: From 2013 to 2017, she served as Ghana's Minister of Education under President John Mahama. During her tenure, she implemented policies aimed at enhancing teacher training and secondary education. Notably, she facilitated scholarships for 10,400 needy students, with a focus on empowering female students. MODERNGHANA.COM

Political Milestones:
In 2020, Professor Opoku-Agyemang was selected as the running mate to John Mahama for the National Democratic Congress (NDC), becoming the first female vice-presidential candidate of a major Ghanaian political party. Their ticket was successful in the 2024 elections, leading to her inauguration as Ghana's first female Vice President on January 7, 2025. GHANAIANTIMES.COM.GH Awards and Recognitions: Her contributions have earned her numerous accolades, including: Officer of the Order of the Volta for Academic Distinction in 2011. Honorary degrees from the University of the West Indies and Winston-Salem State University. Global Leadership Award from the University of South Florida. In 2023, she was listed among the 100 most reputable Africans. EN.WIKIPEDIA.ORG Impact and Legacy: Professor Opoku-Agyemang's career is a testament to her dedication to education and public service. Her leadership at UCC and as Minister of Education has left an indelible mark on Ghana's educational landscape. As Vice President, she continues to inspire and pave the way for future generations of women leaders in Ghana and beyond.
